Our story

Family medicine,
family owned.

Founded in Murphy in 2014 by a husband-and-wife physician team who wanted to practice medicine without a corporate layer between them and their patients. Still independent. Still answering our own phones.

The practice

A clinic shaped by
the people in it.

Medstar Family Care opened its doors in Murphy in 2014 with two exam rooms and a front desk. Our second location in Little Elm followed in 2019. Both clinics are still owned and operated by the founding physicians — no private equity, no hospital system, no regional VP.

That structure shows up in small ways: we book longer visits, we hire slowly, and we make our own schedules. It also shows up in the bigger ones — when a patient needs us on a Saturday or after hours, the answer is usually yes.

Today we serve roughly 12,000 patients across the two locations, with a clinical team that has, on average, been with us for over five years.

What we believe

Six principles, in plain language.

These aren't on a wall plaque — they're how we make decisions when something gets hard.

01

Time is the treatment

Most clinical errors come from rushed visits. We book longer slots so we can listen.

02

Same doctor, every visit

Continuity is medicine. Whenever possible, you see your physician — not whoever is on shift.

03

Answers in the room

On-site labs and imaging mean diagnosis happens during the visit, not three days later.

04

Plain English

Test results, billing, treatment plans — explained the way we'd explain them to family.

05

Prices you can see

Self-pay rates published. No surprise bills. We tell you the cost before we run the test.

06

Independent on purpose

No corporate parent means no productivity quotas. The right care is the goal, not the metric.
