Understanding weight & health

Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial for overall wellbeing. Being overweight or obese is medically defined by Body Mass Index (BMI):

Overweight

BMI 25–29.9

Obese

BMI 30+

Morbid obesity

BMI 40+

Excess weight can contribute to serious health conditions including:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Hypertension (high blood pressure)
  • Heart disease
  • Sleep apnea
  • Joint problems
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Fatty liver disease
  • Increased cancer risk

FAQs

How is medical weight loss different from regular dieting?

Medical weight loss is physician-supervised and addresses the entire spectrum of factors affecting weight, including medical conditions, medications, and metabolic issues. Unlike fad diets, our approach is scientifically sound and tailored to your specific needs and health status.

Am I a good candidate for weight loss medications?

Ideal candidates for medications like semaglutide or tirzepatide typically have a BMI of 30 or higher, or a BMI of 27 or higher with weight-related conditions such as hypertension or type 2 diabetes. Our medical team will evaluate your health status to determine if these treatments are appropriate for you.

Will my insurance cover weight loss treatment?

Many insurance plans now cover obesity screening and counseling. Coverage for medications varies by plan. Our team will help verify your benefits and explore options for maximizing your coverage.

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans, Medicare, and offer flexible payment options. Contact our office to verify your specific coverage.
