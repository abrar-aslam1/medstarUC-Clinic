FAQs

What’s the difference between urgent care and emergency care?

Urgent care is designed for non-life-threatening conditions that need prompt attention, like sprains, minor infections, or flu symptoms. Emergency care handles life-threatening situations such as heart attacks, severe trauma, or stroke. Our urgent care services are more cost-effective and typically have shorter wait times than ERs for non-emergency conditions.

Do I need to make an appointment for urgent care services?

While we accept walk-ins, we recommend booking an appointment to minimize wait times. Same-day appointments are usually available, and you can easily schedule through our website or by calling our office. This helps us ensure you receive timely care while maintaining efficient service for all patients.

What insurance plans do you accept for urgent care visits?

We accept most major insurance plans, and as a primary care facility, your urgent care visit typically involves lower copays compared to emergency room visits. Contact our office for specific insurance verification, and we’ll help you understand your coverage and potential costs.

How long do urgent care visits typically take?

Most urgent care visits at Medstar Family Care are completed within 30–60 minutes, depending on the condition and required treatment. Our integrated approach means you get thorough care without the extended wait times often experienced in emergency rooms.

Can you provide follow-up care after my urgent care visit?

Yes! As a full-service primary care practice, we offer comprehensive follow-up care after your urgent care visit. This integrated approach ensures continuity of care and better health outcomes, setting us apart from standalone urgent care facilities.

What should I bring to my urgent care visit?

Please bring a valid ID, your insurance card, a list of current medications, and any relevant medical records or test results. If you’re experiencing symptoms, it’s helpful to note when they started and any factors that make them better or worse.
